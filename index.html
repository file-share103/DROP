What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is the psychological manipulation of people to perform actions or divulge confidential information.

  • Exploits human psychology rather than technical vulnerabilities
  • Often the first step in larger cyber attacks
  • Can bypass even the strongest technical security measures

Common Attack Vectors

  • Phishing: Fraudulent emails or websites
  • Pretexting: Creating false scenarios
  • Baiting: Offering something enticing
  • Quid Pro Quo: Offering services for information
  • Tailgating: Following authorized personnel

Prevention & Best Practices

Stay Vigilant

Always verify the identity of people requesting sensitive information

Use Security Tools

Employ email filters, antivirus software, and browser security extensions

Education & Training

Regular security awareness training for all organization members

Verify Requests

Use alternative communication channels to verify unusual requests
